6 Ways to Detect Fake News

Apply these checks before sharing anything to ensure accuracy and prevent the spread of misinformation.

🔍
01

Check the Source

Is it a known, credible outlet? Look for about pages, editorial policies, and author bylines. If you haven't heard of the source, use our credibility checker.

Source Verification
📋
02

Verify with 2+ Sources

Real news is reported by multiple outlets. If only one obscure site covers a story, be highly skeptical. Cross-check with Reuters, AP, or BBC.

Cross-Referencing
⚠️
03

Watch for Sensational Headlines

Real journalism rarely uses ALL CAPS, excessive exclamation marks, or emotionally manipulative language. Headlines designed to shock are red flags.

Headline Analysis
📅
04

Check the Date

Old news often recirculates as new, especially on WhatsApp. Always verify the original publication date before sharing or reacting.

Date Verification
🖼️
05

Reverse Image Search

Photos and videos can be taken out of context. Use Google's reverse image search or TinEye to verify where an image originally appeared.

Image Verification
🤔
06

Check Your Own Bias

We tend to believe news that supports our existing views. Before sharing, ask yourself: "Would I question this if it came from the other side?"

Critical Thinking

Types of Misinformation

Not all fake news is the same. Understanding these different categories helps you identify hidden agendas and manipulative patterns.

🚫

Fabricated Content

Completely false stories invented for clicks, profit, or malicious purposes.

High Risk
✂️

Manipulated Content

Real stories or imagery distorted with misleading context or edited elements.

High Risk
🎭

Satire / Parody

Humorous content that gets shared as fact when the comedy context is removed.

Medium Risk
📰

Misleading Headlines

Clickbait titles that don't match or accurately represent the actual article content.

High Risk
📸

False Context

Real photos, videos, or quotes used in the wrong context to support a false claim.

Medium Risk
🕵️

Imposter Content

Websites or social accounts that mimic real news outlets to gain unearned credibility.
