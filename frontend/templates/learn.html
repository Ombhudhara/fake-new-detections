How to Spot Fake News

Misinformation spreads faster than truth. Use these techniques to identify and combat fake news.

🔎
Tip 01
Check the Source
Is it a known, credible outlet? Look for about pages, editorial policies, and author bylines. If you haven't heard of the source, use our credibility checker.
📄
Tip 02
Verify with 2+ Sources
Real news is reported by multiple outlets. If only one obscure site covers a story, be highly skeptical. Cross-check with Reuters, AP, or BBC.
⚠️
Tip 03
Watch for Sensational Headlines
Real journalism rarely uses ALL CAPS, excessive exclamation marks, or emotionally manipulative language. Headlines designed to shock are red flags.
📅
Tip 04
Check the Date
Old news often recirculates as new, especially on WhatsApp. Always verify the original publication date before sharing or reacting.
📷
Tip 05
Reverse Image Search
Photos and videos can be taken out of context. Use Google's reverse image search or TinEye to verify where an image originally appeared.
🤔
Tip 06
Check Your Own Bias
We tend to believe news that supports our existing views. Before sharing, ask yourself: "Would I question this if it came from the other side?"

Types of Misinformation

Not all fake news is the same. Understanding the different types helps identify them:

  • Fabricated Content — completely false stories invented for clicks or profit
  • Manipulated Content — real stories distorted with misleading context or edited images
  • Satire / Parody — humorous content that gets shared without the comedy context
  • Misleading Headlines — clickbait titles that don't match the actual article content
  • False Context — real photos or quotes used in the wrong context entirely
  • Imposter Content — websites that mimic real news outlets to appear credible

Fact-Checking Resources

These organizations verify claims and debunk misinformation professionally:

  • Snopes (snopes.com) — world's oldest and largest fact-checking site
  • AltNews (altnews.in) — India-focused fact-checking in English and Hindi
  • BOOM Live (boomlive.in) — independent Indian digital journalism initiative
  • PolitiFact (politifact.com) — US political fact-checking with a "Truth-O-Meter"
  • Full Fact (fullfact.org) — UK's independent fact checking charity
  • AFP Fact Check (factcheck.afp.com) — global multilingual fact-checking
